Peking University achieved at 1-nm gate length, switching at just 0.6V.
Source →

What This Means

Researchers created transistors with a gate length of just 1 nanometer - roughly 5 silicon atoms wide. Operating at just 0.6 volts means dramatically lower power consumption. Current cutting-edge chips use ~3nm gates. Going to 1nm while maintaining function suggests we're further from the fundamental limits of transistor miniaturization than previously thought - Moore's Law may have more runway than the pessimists claim.
