What This Means

Space-based solar power crossing below $0.10/kWh makes it cost-competitive with terrestrial natural gas ($0.04-0.08/kWh) for the first time. Yesterday's newsletter covered ultralight space solar panels (73g/m²); today's shows the economics are catching up. Combined with falling launch costs from SpaceX, space solar is approaching commercial viability - not as a science experiment, but as actual grid-scale energy.
