Jargon Decoder

Technical terms used in today's issue, explained in plain English.

Hallucinations
When an AI confidently states things that aren't true. Not lying (no intent) but confabulating - filling gaps with plausible-sounding fiction.
HealthBench
A benchmark for evaluating AI performance on clinical medical questions. "Hard" indicates the most difficult subset - questions that require deep medical reasoning.
ARR
Annual Recurring Revenue - the standard metric for subscription software companies. $100M ARR means the company collects $100 million per year from ongoing subscriptions.
18A
Intel's manufacturing process node, roughly equivalent to 1.8 nanometers. Named to compete with TSMC's naming convention. Smaller = more transistors per chip = more performance per watt.
Ferroelectric Transistor
A transistor using ferroelectric materials that can switch states at extremely low voltages. Enables smaller, more energy-efficient chips than traditional silicon transistors.
GLP-1
Glucagon-Like Peptide 1 - a hormone that regulates appetite and blood sugar. Drugs mimicking it (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) cause significant weight loss and are reshaping healthcare economics.
Lithopanspermia
The hypothesis that life can travel between planets on rocks ejected by asteroid impacts. "Litho" = stone, "panspermia" = seeds everywhere. Life hitchhiking across space on debris.
PNAS
Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences - one of the most prestigious peer-reviewed scientific journals. PNAS Nexus is its open-access companion journal.
UAP
Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena - the official US government term replacing "UFO." The rebranding reflects a shift from dismissal to systematic investigation.
Artemis Program
NASA's program to return humans to the Moon. Named after Apollo's twin sister in mythology. Uses SpaceX's Starship as the lunar lander vehicle.
