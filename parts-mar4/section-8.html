What This Means

NASA has pushed the timeline: Artemis III (originally the crewed lunar landing) is now a hardware test flight in 2027, with the actual landing moved to Artemis IV in 2028. While this is a slip from earlier schedules, it still means humans could be back on the Moon - for the first time since 1972 - within two years. SpaceX's Starship is the lander vehicle, tying this directly to SpaceX's accelerating launch cadence from yesterday's newsletter.
