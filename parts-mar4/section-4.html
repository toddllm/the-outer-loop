The CTO of a $100M startup reports zero junior hires since 2024, with senior employees 3x more productive.

What This Means

A startup doing $100 million in annual revenue hasn't hired a single junior employee in over a year. Instead, senior employees are 3x more productive using AI tools. This is one data point, but it aligns with a broader trend: AI is eliminating entry-level knowledge work positions first. The troubling implication: if juniors never get hired, how does the next generation develop the expertise to become seniors?

Gartner predicts AI will create more jobs than it eliminates starting in 2028.
Source →

What This Means

Gartner (the IT research firm whose predictions Fortune 500 companies pay attention to) says AI will be a net job creator by 2028. This is the counterpoint to the "zero junior hires" story above. The argument: while some jobs disappear, new categories emerge (AI trainers, prompt engineers, AI auditors). Whether you find this reassuring depends on whether the new jobs will be accessible to the same people being displaced right now.

OpenAI is developing a rival to GitHub to reduce its reliance on Microsoft.
Source →

What This Means

OpenAI is building its own code hosting platform to compete with GitHub, which is owned by Microsoft - OpenAI's biggest investor and partner. Building a competitor to your own backer's product is a significant strategic signal: OpenAI wants independence from Microsoft's ecosystem. The AI-powered coding market (GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code) is becoming the most lucrative developer tools market in history.
