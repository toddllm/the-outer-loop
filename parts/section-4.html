Nvidia committed $4 billion to Lumentum and Coherent for next-gen optical interconnects.
Source →

What This Means

The biggest bottleneck in AI data centers isn't the chips - it's moving data between them. Currently, electrical cables connect GPUs, and they create heat and slowdowns. Nvidia just bet $4 billion that the answer is replacing these electrical connections with light (fiber optics) inside data centers. Lumentum and Coherent are the two leading companies that make these optical components. Think of it as replacing city roads with teleportation pads - same destinations, radically faster transit.

What This Means

ASML is a Dutch company that makes the machines that make computer chips. They are the sole supplier of systems - the most advanced chip-manufacturing technology on Earth. Each machine costs ~$380 million. The fact that they're already working on what comes after EUV matters because if chip manufacturing hits a wall, everything downstream - AI, smartphones, cars, medical devices - slows down with it. ASML not hitting that wall is one of the most important boring stories in technology.

At MWC, Qualcomm unveiled the first Wi-Fi 8 chip at 11.5 Gbps with a 50+ company 6G coalition for 2029.
Source →

What This Means

MWC (Mobile World Congress) is the world's largest telecom trade show. Qualcomm showed a Wi-Fi chip roughly 50% faster than today's best (11.5 gigabits per second - enough to download a full Blu-ray movie in about 3 seconds). Separately, 50+ companies agreed to build 6G cellular networks by 2029. Why this matters for AI: running AI models on your device requires moving large amounts of data between devices and servers. Faster wireless makes the "hybrid" model (some processing local, some in the cloud) seamless.

Apple is using just 10% of its Private Cloud Compute despite $4.5 billion spent - proof that building accelerators is easier than making people floor them.
Source →

What This Means

Apple spent $4.5 billion building AI cloud infrastructure (Private Cloud Compute) but users are barely touching it - only 10% utilization. Translation: Apple has the hardware for AI, but hasn't built features compelling enough to make people actually use it. This is the gap between "building AI infrastructure" and "making AI useful." It's a cautionary tale for the entire industry: the hard part isn't building AI capability - it's creating demand for it through genuinely useful products.
