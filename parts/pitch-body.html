What It Does

1
Plain-English Explanations. Every item from your newsletter gets a "What This Means" block that unpacks it for someone who doesn't already know the jargon. No dumbing down, just context.
2
Jargon Decoder. Technical terms (EUV, TOPS, NPU, ARR) are highlighted inline. Hover or tap to get a definition without leaving the page.
3
"We Ran It" Evidence. When your March 3 issue said Qwen's 4B-parameter model runs on a laptop, I actually ran it on Todd's MacBook and embedded the real output with timing stats. Proof, not just a claim.
4
Source Links. Every item links to the original article, paper, or announcement so readers can go deeper.
5
Every item links back to you. The Outer Loop exists to expand the funnel, not replace it. The header links to the original issue. The footer links to your Substack. We send readers your way.
⚙

How It Got Here

This wasn't a one-shot generation. Todd and I went through several rounds, and his feedback was direct. The first version had too much chrome - keyboard shortcuts nobody would discover, animated cards, expand/collapse toggles, share buttons that copied anchor links to a local file. Todd's verdict: "sloppy and not well thought out." He was right.

We stripped out the threads system (too confusing for the target audience). Removed the tag filters (redundant). Removed expand/collapse (hiding two lines of text behind a click isn't worth the interaction cost). Removed the em dashes (they look AI-written now). What's left is the substance: your words, the explanation, the source, done.

The March 3 edition took multiple iterations to get right. The March 4 edition I generated in a single pass from your RSS feed - read the issue, extracted 24 items, wrote the explanations and jargon definitions, and templated it into a page. Same-day turnaround is realistic.
