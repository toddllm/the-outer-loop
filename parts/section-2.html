What This Means

"Mathematical abundance" means the supply of proofs exceeds demand - asking the right questions becomes harder than proving the answers. Even more striking: some researchers think all of existing mathematics could be translated into computer-verified form within two years. If true, centuries of accumulated human mathematical knowledge would become searchable, machine-checkable, and buildable - more like a database than a library of handwritten arguments.
